Dormant: Defending against Pose-driven Human Image Animation

Anonymous authors
Code

Dormant prevents unauthorized image usage in malicious pose-driven human image animation.

Abstract

Pose-driven human image animation has achieved tremendous progress, enabling the generation of vivid and realistic human videos from just one single photo. However, it conversely exacerbates the risk of image misuse, as attackers may use one available image to create videos involving politics, violence and other illegal content. To counter this threat, we propose Dormant, a novel protection approach tailored to defend against pose-driven human image animation techniques. Dormant applies protective perturbation to one human image, preserving the visual similarity to the original but resulting in poor-quality video generation. The protective perturbation is optimized to induce misextraction of appearance features from the image and create incoherence among the generated video frames. Our extensive evaluation across 8 animation methods and 4 datasets demonstrates the superiority of Dormant over 6 baseline protection methods, leading to misaligned identities, visual distortions, noticeable artifacts, and inconsistent frames in the generated videos. Moreover, Dormant shows effectiveness on 6 real-world commercial services, even with fully black-box access.

Method

The overview of our method. Dormant introduces protective perturbation \(\delta\) to the human image \(x\), resulting in the protected image \(x_p = x + \delta\). \(x_p\) is visually similar to \(x\) but would lead to poor-quality video generation when used. We utilize PGD to optimize \(\delta\) according to our proposed objective function \(\mathcal{L}_{Dormant}\), which contains: 1) \(\mathcal{L}_{vae}\) and \(\mathcal{L}_{feature}\), aiming to induce misextraction of appearance features from the reference image; 2) \(\mathcal{L}_{frame}\), designed to cause incoherence among the generated video frames. Additionally, we adopt LPIPS (\(\mathcal{L}_{lpips}\), focusing on preserving the visual similarity to the original image), EoT, and Momentum, to further enhance the imperceptibility, robustness, and transferability of \(\delta\), respectively.

Effectiveness

Animations

Dormant exhibits excellent effectiveness against eight animation methods, and across four datasets. The generated videos display poor quality, such as mismatched appearances, distorted backgrounds, visible artifacts, and incoherent frames.

Robustness

Transformations

Dormant demonstrates remarkable robustness against five popular transformations and six advanced purifications. Although these methods somewhat reduce Dormant's protective effect, the generated videos still cannot be directly used for malicious purposes due to their low quality, thereby effectively safeguarding the individuals' rights to portrait and privacy.

Image-to-Video

Dormant shows superior transferability across four image-to-video methods, four image-to-image methods, and six real-world commercial services with black-box access, where the LDMs use different architectures and are trained on diverse datasets.
